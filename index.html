Welcome to Switzerland

Switzerland is famous for its Alps, lakes, chocolates, watches, and peaceful towns. This page collects highlights your class can use for a presentation.

  • Multilingual nation (DE / FR / IT / RM)
  • Beautiful landscapes & clean cities
  • Excellent public transport
  • Rich cultural festivals

Tourism & Famous Places

Top places students should know:

Zermatt (Matterhorn)

Famous mountain peak and ski destination. The village is car-free.

Lucerne

Known for Chapel Bridge, old town, and lake cruises.

Interlaken & Jungfraujoch

Adventure hub between lakes; gateway to Jungfraujoch — "Top of Europe".

Food & Clothing

Popular Foods

  • Fondue — melted cheese served with bread for dipping.
  • Raclette — melted cheese scraped over potatoes and pickles.
  • Rösti — crispy potato dish, often a side.

Traditional Clothing

Traditional Swiss costumes vary by region. They often include embroidered blouses or shirts, vests, and decorative hats for festivals. In Alpine areas, people also wear practical wool clothing and leather boots.

Transportation

Switzerland has an efficient transport system which makes travelling easy for visitors and locals alike.

Trains

Extensive rail network — scenic trains like Glacier Express and Bernina Express are famous worldwide.

Buses & Trams

Cities have reliable tram and bus services. Regional buses connect villages and towns.

Cable Cars & Mountain Trains

Used to reach mountain viewpoints and skiing areas — a must for tourism.

Culture & Traditions

Swiss culture combines German, French, Italian and Romansh influences. People value punctuality, cleanliness, and respect for nature.

  • Languages: German (majority), French, Italian, Romansh.
  • Music & Dance: Folk music, yodeling, and traditional dances at local events.
  • Architecture: Wooden chalets in the mountains, medieval buildings in old towns.

Festivals & Currency

Major Festivals

  • Montreux Jazz Festival (summer) — one of the world’s best music festivals.
  • Basel Fasnacht — famous carnival with masks and parades (Feb/Mar).
  • Sechseläuten — spring festival in Zurich featuring a snowman-like "Böögg" burning.

Currency

Switzerland uses the Swiss Franc (CHF). Notes are colorful and coins come in various sizes. Many places accept cards, but keep some cash for small shops or mountain areas.

Famous Personalities & Monuments

Albert Einstein

Lived in Bern (1902–1909) while he developed parts of his theory of relativity. Bern has an Einstein Museum and a house you can visit.

Roger Federer

One of the greatest tennis players ever — Swiss sports icon.

Lion Monument (Lucerne)

A famous carving commemorating Swiss Guards who died during the French Revolution — described by Mark Twain as "the most mournful and moving piece of stone in the world."
